# Keyword Research SOP for GTM Strategy

## Purpose

This section provides a structured process for identifying, analyzing, and prioritizing keywords that will form the foundation of the client's content strategy, SEO efforts, and paid search campaigns. Effective keyword research connects the client's Unique Value Proposition (UVP) and offerings to the language and search behavior of their Ideal Customer Profile (ICP).

## Process Overview

1. Initial Keyword Discovery
2. Search Intent Analysis
3. Competitive Keyword Analysis
4. Keyword Prioritization
5. Keyword Grouping and Mapping
6. Implementation Recommendations

## Detailed Process Steps

### 1. Initial Keyword Discovery

**Objective**: Generate a comprehensive list of potential keywords based on the client's offerings, UVP, and customer language.

**Input Resources**:
- Completed UVP document
- Completed ICP document
- Client's existing marketing materials
- Client's product/service catalog
- Customer interviews and testimonials (if available)

**Process**:

A. **UVP-Derived Keywords**
- Extract key phrases from the UVP that highlight unique benefits
- List industry-specific terminology mentioned in the UVP
- Identify problem statements the UVP addresses

B. **ICP-Derived Keywords**
- List terms and phrases used by the ICP when describing their challenges
- Identify industry-specific terminology used by the ICP
- Note differences in language between different ICP segments

C. **Product/Service-Based Keywords**
- List all products/services offered by the client
- For each product/service, identify:
  - Generic terms (e.g., "architectural design")
  - Specific features (e.g., "sustainable architectural design")
  - Benefits (e.g., "energy-efficient building design")
  - Problem-solution pairings (e.g., "reducing carbon footprint in commercial buildings")

D. **Expanded Keyword Discovery**
- Use keyword research tools to expand initial list:
  - Google Keyword Planner
  - SEMrush
  - Ahrefs
  - Moz Keyword Explorer
  - AnswerThePublic
- Research related searches and "People also ask" sections from Google
- Examine autocomplete suggestions for seed keywords

**Output**: Raw keyword list with at least 200-300 potential keywords/phrases

### 2. Search Intent Analysis

**Objective**: Understand the purpose behind each keyword search to align content and offers with user expectations.

**Process**:

A. **Classify Keywords by Search Intent**
- Informational Intent (seeking information): How-to queries, definition queries, comparative queries
- Navigational Intent (looking for a specific site): Brand terms, product names
- Commercial Investigation Intent (researching before purchase): Reviews, comparisons, "best" queries
- Transactional Intent (ready to purchase): Buy/purchase terms, price-related terms, location-specific service terms

B. **Search Results Analysis**
- For top 20-30 keywords, analyze the first page of search results
- Document the content types ranking (blogs, product pages, etc.)
- Note featured snippets and other SERP features
- Identify content formats that perform well (guides, lists, videos, etc.)

**Output**: Keywords categorized by search intent with notes on SERP characteristics

### 3. Competitive Keyword Analysis

**Objective**: Identify keyword opportunities based on competitor strengths and weaknesses.

**Process**:

A. **Identify Top Competitors** — Direct competitors (3-5), indirect competitors (2-3), content competitors (sites ranking for target keywords)

B. **Analyze Competitor Keyword Performance**
- Keywords competitors rank for (top 100 positions)
- Keywords where competitors have featured snippets
- High-performing content topics
- Keyword gaps (terms competitors don't target)

C. **Difficulty Assessment**
- Evaluate ranking difficulty for high-value keywords
- Identify low-competition opportunities
- Document authority metrics for ranking sites

**Output**: Competitive keyword landscape analysis with identified opportunities

### 4. Keyword Prioritization

**Objective**: Rank keywords based on their potential value to the client's business objectives.

**Criteria**:
- Search volume
- Business relevance (scale of 1-5)
- Competition level
- Conversion potential
- Alignment with UVP
- Alignment with ICP needs
- Current ranking position (if applicable)

**Segment by Timeframe**:
- Short-term opportunities (3-6 months)
- Mid-term targets (6-12 months)
- Long-term ambitions (12+ months)

**Output**: Prioritized keyword list with scoring rationale

### 5. Keyword Grouping and Mapping

**Objective**: Organize keywords into logical groups to guide content creation and site architecture.

A. **Thematic Grouping** — Group related keywords by topic, identify primary (head) and secondary (long-tail) terms, create topic clusters

B. **Customer Journey Mapping** — Align keyword groups with stages: Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Retention/Advocacy

C. **Site Structure Alignment** — Map keyword groups to website sections, identify content gaps, recommend new pages

**Output**: Keyword groupings with customer journey alignment and site mapping

### 6. Implementation Recommendations

A. **Content Recommendations** — Priority content pieces, content types by keyword group, refresh opportunities

B. **On-Page SEO Guidelines** — Keyword placement, meta data templates, internal linking strategy

C. **Paid Search Recommendations** — High-value PPC keywords, ad group structure, budget allocation

**Output**: Implementation plan with specific next steps

## Documentation Requirements

1. Executive Summary: Overview of approach and key findings
2. Complete Keyword Database: Full list with metrics
3. Priority Keyword List: Top opportunities with rationale
4. Keyword Maps: Visual representation of groups and journey alignment
5. Implementation Roadmap: Phased approach for content and campaigns

## Tips for Effective Keyword Research

1. Focus on quality over quantity — prioritize relevance over pure search volume
2. Consider the full funnel — include keywords for all journey stages
3. Look beyond obvious terms — uncover niche long-tail keywords with high intent
4. Analyze actual search results — don't rely solely on tool data
5. Update regularly — refresh quarterly for emerging trends
6. Consider seasonality — note keywords with seasonal fluctuations
7. Balance ambition with reality — target achievable keywords alongside aspirational ones

## Integration with Other GTM Components

- **UVP**: Ensures keyword targeting reflects unique differentiation
- **ICP**: Aligns keyword selection with target audience language
- **Offer Development**: Connects keywords to specific offers and conversion paths
- **Content Strategy**: Provides foundation for topic selection and content planning
- **Paid Media**: Guides efficient budget allocation for paid search campaigns
- **Analytics & Measurement**: Establishes KPIs for organic search performance
